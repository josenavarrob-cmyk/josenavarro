Most teams adopt AI through tools before they adopt it through culture. That is understandable. Tools are concrete. Culture is slow.

But there is an early mistake many organizations make: they confuse delegation with leadership.

> Software can distribute tasks. It cannot absorb uncertainty for other people.

Managers do several jobs at once. They make tradeoffs with incomplete information, hold emotional context, and shape how people interpret change. AI can support each of these functions, but it cannot own them.

The practical question is not whether AI can replace a manager. The practical question is how much managerial energy can be redirected from repetitive coordination toward clearer decision-making.

If AI gives leaders time back, that time should be reinvested in the work only humans can do well: coaching, alignment, and moral clarity.
