Speed is frequently treated as a neutral good in modern organizations. It is not.

Speed creates pressure gradients. Decisions are made with less context. Explanations become thinner. Small misunderstandings calcify into broader narratives about who is heard and who is not.

In these moments, emotional labor is not a soft skill. It is an operating requirement.

People still need to be oriented, reassured, and challenged. They need language for uncertainty. They need to know what is changing, what is stable, and why.

When leaders fail to provide that language, teams generate their own. Informal narratives fill the vacuum, and trust becomes expensive to rebuild.

Sustainable speed is not just a product problem. It is a social design problem.
